Welcome to CAPS4Kids!

Founder of CAPS4Kids

Hello Everyone! I'm Suraj Chigati, the founder of Chess and Programming For Kids. I'm currently a high school student at Lynbrook, and I am studying Computer Science. I created CAPS4Kids to bring the chess and programming communities of my home town together to create something absolutely remarkable! As for my credentials, I have a United States Chess Federation rating near 1200 and am extremely proficient in Python and Java. Students who participate in this program are guaranteed to benefit, whether it may be to learn new skills in programming and chess or to hone their existing programming and chess skills!

CAPS4Kids 2017

In the summer of 2017, we had 6 students who participated in our summer camp program. Our summer class had 10 weeks on instruction for both chess and java. We separated our classes into three different divisions: beginner, intermediate, and advanced. Our students learned the basics of coding and also learned how to play chess competitively. After the 10 weeks, we were pleased to see that all our students had learned a lot about chess and java and have vastly improved their skills.

CAPS4Kids 2018

In the summer of 2018, we had 15 students who participated in our summer camp program. The students gained hands-on experience with MIT App Inventor and were able to develop tons of applications, ranging from simple projects such as sound players to more complicated game designs. The students also learned how to play chess, and how to operate in a chess tournament. But the most important part of the summer camp was that our students were able to interact with one another cohesively and enjoyed the classes.

CAPS4Kids 2019

In the summer of 2019, we partnered with MathEdge and 10 students participated in our summer camp. Our students were able to learn how to solve simple and complicated programming topics in java, such as string manipulation, data storing with arrays, and were also able to develop their own programs. Our students also learned how to use www.chess.com and play online matches with other students. We are proud at how much CAPS4Kids has grown since its inception in 2016, and are hoping to continue this for many years to come!
